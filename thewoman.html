The Verdict

The Woman in Cabin 10 (2025) tries to deliver a slick psychological thriller aboard a luxury yacht, with an engrossing setup and a stellar cast. However, its execution falls short—slick visuals and strong talent struggle to overcome a plot that many find contrived and uninspired.

Plot Summary

Journalist Laura “Lo” Blacklock (Keira Knightley) boards a luxury super-yacht for a work assignment. One night she witnesses a woman being thrown overboard, yet the next morning all passengers are accounted for and no one believes her. As she investigates further, she risks her career—and her life.

What Works

The concept is enticing: a closed-door mystery at sea with elite characters, isolation and paranoia. Knightley anchors the film with charisma, and the yacht setting brings a polished, glamorous visual layer to the tension. The deceptive calm of luxury contrasted with hidden danger gives the film some atmospheric strength.

What Doesn't Work

Despite the promising premise, the narrative often feels thin and predictable. The pacing drags, and many viewers found the characters’ decisions implausible. The film’s final act accelerates in a way that undercuts suspense rather than builds it.

Final Thoughts

If you’re in the mood for a stylish, surface-level thriller and enjoy the luxury-yacht backdrop and cast, The Woman in Cabin 10 may offer light entertainment. But if you favour tight plotting, strong logic and high suspense, this adaptation may leave you disappointed.
