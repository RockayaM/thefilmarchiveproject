The Verdict

Frankenstein (2025) is a haunting and visually stunning reimagining of the classic tale. Guillermo del Toro delivers a rich, emotional story that blends gothic horror and tragic beauty. While breathtaking in scope, its slow pacing and artistic flourishes may not appeal to everyone.

Plot Summary

Dr. Victor Frankenstein, driven by obsession, creates life from death—only to unleash a creature that reflects his own guilt and loneliness. As the monster seeks understanding, both creator and creation spiral toward inevitable tragedy.

What Works

Del Toro’s direction shines with dark elegance, and the performances—especially from Oscar Isaac and Jacob Elordi—bring deep emotion to the story. The visuals and atmosphere create a powerful, immersive experience.

What Doesn't Work

The film’s pacing can feel slow, and its deviations from the original story may frustrate traditionalists. Its emotional depth sometimes comes at the expense of tension.

Final Thoughts

A beautifully crafted, melancholy masterpiece, Frankenstein (2025) redefines a timeless story with heart and grandeur. Not for all tastes, but unforgettable for those who embrace its haunting vision.
