The Verdict

The Gorge (2025) is a bold, genre-bending mix of action, sci-fi, and romance from director Scott Derrickson. With striking visuals, emotional tension, and strong performances from Miles Teller and Anya Taylor-Joy, it delivers a fresh spin on the isolated-soldier thriller. Though it sometimes struggles with pacing and tone, its ambition and heart make it stand out from the crowd.

Plot Summary

Set in a desolate, futuristic landscape, the story follows Levi (Miles Teller) and Drasa (Anya Taylor-Joy), two elite snipers stationed on opposite sides of a massive gorge. Their mission is simple but haunting: prevent whatever lies below from escaping. Over time, their daily communications evolve from terse military exchanges to something deeply personal. But when an ancient, monstrous threat rises from the depths, the pair must cross enemy lines and descend into the gorge itself—confronting not just the horror beneath, but the truth about why they were sent there.

What Works

The film’s central concept—a forbidden connection formed across a deadly divide—is both intimate and cinematic. Derrickson’s direction brings scale and tension, turning the gorge into a living, breathing entity. Teller and Taylor-Joy share believable chemistry that grounds the film’s stranger elements in real emotion. The cinematography is stunning, with vast desert vistas and eerie subterranean sequences that heighten the sense of mystery. When the action erupts, it’s intense and visually spectacular, balancing thrills with a surprisingly emotional core.

What Doesn't Work

Despite its strong foundation, The Gorge occasionally stumbles. The pacing is uneven, with a slow, meditative first half that gives way to a chaotic, fast-cut finale. Some character backstories feel underdeveloped, leaving emotional beats that don’t fully land. The shifts between romance, sci-fi mystery, and monster horror can feel abrupt, and certain plot twists are more predictable than profound. While the movie’s ambition is admirable, it sometimes reaches further than it can grasp.

Final Thoughts

The Gorge isn’t flawless, but it’s a daring and visually stunning ride that blends heart, horror, and spectacle in equal measure. With compelling leads and an atmosphere thick with tension, it offers a unique cinematic experience that lingers long after the credits. It may not redefine its genres, but it proves that even in familiar territory, bold storytelling can still surprise and move us.
