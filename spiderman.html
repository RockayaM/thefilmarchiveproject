The Verdict

A visually groundbreaking and emotionally ambitious sequel, Across the Spider-Verse raises the bar for animated filmmaking while deepening the heart and complexity of Miles Morales’s story.

Plot Summary

A year after saving the multiverse, Miles Morales continues to juggle life as a student and a superhero. When Gwen Stacy reappears, he’s drawn into a vast network of Spider-People known as the Spider-Society, who protect the fabric of the multiverse. But when Miles uncovers a hidden truth about his destiny, he must defy the system and fight for his own story — even if it means standing alone.

What Works

The animation is nothing short of revolutionary, blending styles, textures, and colors to create a living comic book that bursts with creativity. The emotional weight is genuine — Miles’s struggle for identity, belonging, and independence resonates powerfully. Voice performances from Shameik Moore and Hailee Steinfeld shine, and the film’s score and pacing build incredible energy and tension throughout.

What Doesn't Work

The dense multiverse plot can feel overwhelming at times, and the cliffhanger ending may leave some viewers unsatisfied until the next installment arrives. A few supporting Spider-characters are visually striking but underexplored.

Final Thoughts

Across the Spider-Verse is an exhilarating mix of art and emotion — a sequel that doesn’t just repeat success but redefines what animated storytelling can be. It’s bold, heartfelt, and leaves you eager for the next chapter in Miles Morales’s extraordinary journey.
