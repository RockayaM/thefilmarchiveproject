The Verdict

Heads of State (2025) is a high-energy action-comedy that leans hard into global mayhem and buddy-cop dynamic—only this time the cops are world leaders. With John Cena playing a larger-than-life U.S. President and Idris Elba as the stoic U.K. Prime Minister, the film delivers brisk entertainment, though it doesn’t dig much deeper than the fireworks.

Plot Summary

President Will Derringer (John Cena) and British Prime Minister Sam Clarke (Idris Elba) share a public rivalry that threatens their countries’ alliance. During a joint diplomatic trip, disaster strikes—a surprise attack forces the two leaders into a desperate escape across hostile terrain. They’re hunted by a ruthless arms-dealer’s henchmen while a global conspiracy unravels, and the unlikely pair must set aside their differences to survive and stop the threat.

What Works

The film thrives when it embraces its absurdity. Cena and Elba play nicely off one another—the brash populist action-star-turned-President and the no-nonsense veteran Prime Minister create a fun dynamic. The action set pieces—chases, hand-to-hand, explosions—are polished and carry the movie. And the international scope adds spectacle: from Eastern Europe to covert offices, the film uses its locations. Critics and audiences alike note that while the plot is simple, the film is “stupid fun” in the best sense.

What Doesn't Work

However, the story doesn’t offer many surprises and the characters outside the two leads are thinly sketched. The tone shifts between comedic banter and high stakes action sometimes feel uneven. The film’s rhetoric on leadership and international politics remains superficial—if you’re looking for depth, you may come away wanting more.

Final Thoughts

Heads of State isn’t aiming for Oscar-bait—it’s built as a breezy, globe-trotting action ride with charismatic stars at its core. If you’re after sharp political drama or clever satire, this won’t satisfy; but if you just want to sit back, watch the world go to hell while two big personalities crack jokes and shoot bad guys, you’ll have a good time.
